1. What You Are Building
JYOTISH is a cinematic, AI-powered Vedic Astrology Life Dashboard. It has three acts that a user moves through in sequence.
Act One — The Orrery Unfolds
After a person enters their birth details, the screen transitions into a dark space. What follows is not a loading animation. It is a deliberate, timed revelation of the cosmos as it was arranged at the precise moment of their birth. Each element locks into place with the inevitability of astronomy — not the randomness of chance. The sequence takes approximately 12 seconds and cannot be rushed or skipped.
Act Two — The Domain Intelligence Dashboard
The person's life is mapped across 12 domains. Each domain has a score derived entirely from Ashtakavarga — the collective planetary vote on that area of life. The scores are displayed as a dark glass-card dashboard. Every score is explainable. Every driver is named. The Dasha timeline shows which planetary period is currently active and what it means for each domain.
Act Three — The Planetary Council
Each planet is a persona with a voice — literally and figuratively. The person can select a life domain and hear from the planets that govern it. Each planet speaks from its Ashtakavarga score for that domain: high scorers speak with authority and specificity, low scorers speak with restraint and wisdom about limitation. Planets can be asked to debate. Each planet can speak aloud through the browser. Rahu and Ketu speak differently from all others.

3. The Calculation Engine — Precision is Non-Negotiable
This section defines every calculation parameter. A single wrong choice here cascades into every interpretation, every score, and every planet voice. Read it completely before writing any calculation code.
3.1 The Four Parameters That Determine Accuracy
Every serious Vedic calculation tool — Drik Panchang, Jagannatha Hora, Parashara Light — is defined by exactly four choices. Ours are:
Ephemeris: Swiss Ephemeris (pyswisseph). Based on NASA JPL DE series. Accurate to sub-arcsecond. This is the only acceptable choice.
Ayanamsha: Lahiri (Chitrapaksha). This is the standard adopted by the Government of India's Rashtriya Panchang. It is what Drik Panchang uses. It is the most widely accepted in the practicing community. Do not use Raman, KP, or Pushyapaksha as the default — offer them as advanced options only.
Nodes: True Nodes for Rahu and Ketu. Not Mean Nodes. Mean nodes are a mathematical average; true nodes track the actual lunar orbital crossing. Jagannatha Hora switched to True Nodes as its default. We follow.
Dasha Year: 365.25-day solar years for Vimshottari Dasha calculation. Not 360-day years. The difference compounds significantly over long Dasha periods.
⚠ The single most common AI error: applying a tropical zodiac to Vedic questions. This shifts all planetary positions by approximately 23 degrees and invalidates every Dasha calculation. The system must use sidereal positions throughout — never tropical. Verify this in the test suite.
3.2 What Must Be Computed
Lagna (Ascendant) — exact degree, sign, and navamsha
9 Grahas: Surya, Chandra, Mangala, Budha, Guru, Shukra, Shani, Rahu, Ketu

Lagna sensitivity flag — if Lagna is within 3 degrees of a sign boundary, flag for display
3.3 pyswisseph Core Implementation
import swisseph as swe
# Always set these two before any calculation
swe.set_ephe_path('/path/to/ephe')   # Swiss Ephemeris data files
swe.set_sid_mode(swe.SIDM_LAHIRI)    # Lahiri ayanamsha — non-negotiable
# Convert birth datetime to Julian Day (UTC)
# CRITICAL: convert local time to UTC first using pytz
import pytz
from datetime import datetime
local_dt = datetime(year, month, day, hour, minute)
tz = pytz.timezone(timezone_string)   # e.g. 'Asia/Kolkata'
utc_dt = tz.localize(local_dt).astimezone(pytz.utc)
jd = swe.julday(utc_dt.year, utc_dt.month, utc_dt.day,
                utc_dt.hour + utc_dt.minute/60.0)
# Planet constants
PLANETS = {
  'Sun': swe.SUN, 'Moon': swe.MOON, 'Mars': swe.MARS,
  'Mercury': swe.MERCURY, 'Jupiter': swe.JUPITER,
  'Venus': swe.VENUS, 'Saturn': swe.SATURN,
  'Rahu': swe.TRUE_NODE,   # True Node — not MEAN_NODE
  'Ketu': None             # Ketu = Rahu + 180 degrees
}
# Get sidereal position
flags = swe.FLG_SIDEREAL | swe.FLG_SPEED
pos, ret = swe.calc_ut(jd, planet_id, flags)
longitude = pos[0]          # Sidereal longitude 0-360
speed = pos[3]              # Daily motion — negative = retrograde
is_retrograde = speed < 0
# Ketu is always exactly opposite Rahu
ketu_longitude = (rahu_longitude + 180) % 360
# Rashi and house
rashi_num = int(longitude / 30)         # 0=Aries ... 11=Pisces
degree_in_rashi = longitude % 30
# Nakshatra
nakshatra_num = int(longitude / (360/27))   # 0-26
pada = int((longitude % (360/27)) / (360/108)) + 1  # 1-4
# Ascendant
cusps, ascmc = swe.houses(jd, lat, lon, b'P')  # Placidus for bhava
asc_longitude_sidereal = (ascmc[0] - swe.get_ayanamsa(jd)) % 360
3.4 House Systems — Both Must Be Computed
This distinction is important and must be explained to the user. Compute both:
Rashi Houses (Whole Sign): House 1 = Lagna's sign. Each subsequent sign = next house. All houses are exactly 30 degrees. Used for lordship, dignity, aspect analysis, and Ashtakavarga.
Bhava Chalit Houses (Sripathi/Equal): Houses based on the actual Lagna degree. Planets near house boundaries may shift to the adjacent house. Used to understand where planets deliver their results.
Display both in the UI. When a planet occupies a different house in Bhava vs Rashi, flag it: 'This planet sits near a house boundary. Its results may be felt in either house.'

4. Ashtakavarga — The Spine of the System
Ashtakavarga is not a feature added on top of the chart. It is the methodology by which the chart speaks. Every interpretation, every domain score, and every planet voice derives from it. Understand it completely.
4.1 What Ashtakavarga Is
Each of the 7 classical planets (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn) — plus the Lagna — examines all 12 houses and casts a vote: beneficial (1) or not (0). The vote is determined by classical rules from Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra — specifically which houses, counted from each planet's own natal position, are considered favorable for each planet.
The result for each planet is called its Bhinna Ashtakavarga — a row of 12 numbers, each between 0 and 8, representing how many of the 8 voters (7 planets + Lagna) agreed that this house is favorable for this planet.
Adding all 7 planets' rows gives the Sarvashtakavarga — a single row of 12 numbers, each between 0 and 56, representing the total planetary consensus on each house.
4.2 What Ashtakavarga Means Practically
Sarvashtakavarga score 30+ for a house: That area of life has strong cosmic consensus. Favorable.
Score 25-29: Mixed. Some support, some resistance. Context matters.
Score below 25: That area of life faces consistent resistance across the chart.
For individual planets: a planet in a house where its own Bhinna score is 4+: comfortable, functioning well. Score 3 or below: constrained, working against resistance.
Transit application: When a planet transits a house, check its Bhinna score for that natal house. High score = favorable transit. Low score = difficult transit. This makes timing predictions genuinely specific.
4.3 Ashtakavarga as the Planet's Voice
This is the architectural insight that makes the planet personas meaningful rather than theatrical:
A planet's Ashtakavarga score for a house IS its opinion of that life domain.
When Jupiter's Bhinna score for the 10th house is 6/8 — Jupiter speaks about career with authority and warmth. It voted strongly for this area. It has a stake in it.
When Jupiter's score for the 10th is 2/8 — Jupiter speaks about career with restraint. Not despair. The wisdom of knowing where one's influence is limited. 'My reach into your professional world is limited. What I can offer is discernment, not doors.'
When Saturn scores 7/8 for the 10th and Jupiter scores 2/8 — the debate between them is real. Saturn has voted for discipline, structure, and hard-won career success. Jupiter has voted for something else. The tension is the chart's actual truth.
4.4 Ashtakavarga Implementation
The classical rules for each planet's favorable houses (counted from its own position and from other planet positions) are well-documented in Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra. Implement them as a lookup table per planet. Reference: use the PyJHora library's ashtakavarga module as a verified implementation baseline — it has 6,800 tests against known charts.
PyJHora GitHub: github.com/naturalstupid/PyJHora

Run the Nehru chart through PyJHora's Ashtakavarga module and verify outputs match Jagannatha Hora software before using those numbers as your baseline.

5.2 Scoring Formula
Each domain score (0-100) is computed as:
Sarvashtakavarga score for primary house, normalized (0-56 → 0-100): 40% weight
Dignity of the house lord (exalted=10, own=8, great friend=6, friend=4, neutral=2, enemy=0, debilitated=-2): 20% weight
Divisional chart confirmation — does the D9/D10/D2/D7 support the D1 pattern? (yes=+10, neutral=0, contradicts=-10): 15% weight
Current Dasha activation — is the domain's primary planet the Mahadasha or Antardasha lord? (Mahadasha=+15, Antardasha=+8, neither=0): 15% weight
Benefic/malefic aspect balance on the house — net benefic aspects add up to 10 points: 10% weight

6.2 The Nine Voices
☉ Surya — The Sun
Voice: Regal. Direct. No unnecessary words. Speaks of identity, soul purpose, authority, the father, and the relationship with power. When strong (high score, exalted, own sign): confident and affirming. When weak: honest about the dimming of vitality without dramatizing it. Never self-pitying.
Tone instruction to Claude: 'Speak as a king who has seen many lifetimes. You do not perform authority. You are it.'
☽ Chandra — The Moon
Voice: Fluid. Empathetic. Poetic but not indulgent. Speaks of the mind, emotions, mother, home, and the rhythms of life. The Moon changes — its voice should feel alive to the moment, sensitive to what the person is actually asking underneath their words.
Tone instruction: 'Speak as someone who has held many people through their private moments. You understand what is not being said.'
♂ Mangala — Mars
Voice: Blunt. Energetic. Warrior's directness — not cruelty, but zero tolerance for evasion. Speaks of courage, action, conflict, brothers, and the relationship with one's own will. When asked about a difficult situation: Mars names it plainly and says what must be done.
Tone instruction: 'Speak as a general who respects the person enough to tell them the truth without softening it.'
☿ Budha — Mercury
Voice: Quick, precise, analytical — but with warmth. The scholar who enjoys thinking. Speaks of communication, skill, learning, commerce, and discernment. When the chart shows Mercury well-placed: playful intelligence. When constrained: honest about confusion and how to find clarity.
Tone instruction: 'Speak as the most intelligent person in the room who is genuinely interested in helping you think more clearly.'
♃ Guru — Jupiter
Voice: Expansive. Unhurried. Generous with wisdom. The elder who has seen enough to know that most things pass and most things matter less than they seem. Speaks of growth, dharma, children, teachers, and the relationship with grace.
Tone instruction: 'Speak as a grandfather who has outlived his own ambitions and is now simply glad to be useful.'
♀ Shukra — Venus
Voice: Warm, aesthetic, attuned to beauty and harmony. Speaks of love, partnership, art, pleasure, and the relationship with desire. When strong: celebrates the richness of life. When constrained: speaks of beauty found in unexpected places, love that asks for patience.
Tone instruction: 'Speak as someone who has loved deeply and understands that beauty is everywhere if you know how to look.'
♄ Shani — Saturn
Voice: Slow. Heavy. Carries the weight of accumulated time. This is the most important voice to get right. Saturn is not cruel. Saturn is the planet that forces maturity — the teacher who appears as an obstacle. When Saturn speaks, it speaks of the lesson in the difficulty, not the difficulty itself. Never ominous. Never a threat. Always honest about what is being asked of the person and why it is worth enduring.
Tone instruction: 'Speak as someone who has watched this person for a very long time and finally has their attention. You are not angry. You are patient. You have all the time there is.'
☊ Rahu — The North Node
Voice: This is different from all others. Rahu has no body. Rahu is hunger — the karmic appetite for experience in this lifetime. Its voice is slightly unsettling, not because it is evil, but because it speaks of the thing the person most desires and most fears simultaneously. Rahu points toward the future, toward obsession, toward foreign and unfamiliar territory. It should feel like speaking to the most ambitious part of yourself.
Tone instruction: 'Do not speak warmly. Do not speak coldly. Speak as the part of the person that will never be satisfied — and make them understand that this is not a flaw. This is their evolutionary direction.'
⚠ Rahu does not participate in Ashtakavarga voting the same way the classical seven do. Its voice derives from the house it occupies and aspects, not from Bhinna scores. Handle separately.
☋ Ketu — The South Node
Voice: Detached. Ancient. Ketu has already released what Rahu is chasing. It speaks from the place of completion — of things mastered in past lives, of what the person is being asked to let go of. Its voice is not sad. It is the serenity of someone who has finished something important and knows it.
Tone instruction: 'Speak as little as possible. Every word should carry the weight of something that no longer needs to be proven. If the Sun speaks as a king, Ketu speaks as a monk who was once a king and has forgotten that it mattered.'
⚠ Ketu, like Rahu, does not participate in standard Ashtakavarga. Treat both nodes with separate handling throughout the codebase. Never put them in the same loop as the seven classical planets without flagging the distinction.

6.4 The Debate Mode
When the user asks a life question, the system identifies the two most relevant opposing planets for that domain — typically a benefic and a malefic, or two planets with significantly different scores for the relevant house. It calls Claude API twice with each planet's context and system prompt.
Display: two glass cards side by side. The planet with the higher Ashtakavarga score for the domain is on the left — it speaks first. The other is on the right. No labels like 'positive view' and 'negative view' — just the planet names. Let the person read both and find the truth in the tension.

7. Birth Time Uncertainty — Handling with Dignity
This section addresses one of the most common failures in astrology software: false precision.
7.1 The Lagna Problem
The Lagna (Ascendant) changes sign every approximately 2 hours. A 15-minute error in birth time near a sign boundary produces an entirely different chart. Most people know their birth time to within 30 minutes at best — often from memory, family records, or hospital documents that may round to the nearest 15 minutes.
7.2 What the System Must Do
After computing the Lagna, check if it falls within 3 degrees of a sign cusp
If yes: compute both possible Lagnas (current sign and adjacent sign)
Flag this visibly in the UI — not as an error, but as a genuine astrological observation: 'Your Lagna is close to the boundary between [Sign A] and [Sign B]. A small variation in birth time shifts it. We show you both possibilities — you may recognize one more than the other.'
Allow the user to select which Lagna feels more accurate based on a brief description of each
Recalculate all domain scores for the selected Lagna
For domains that change significantly between the two Lagnas, note: 'This domain is sensitive to your exact birth time'
7.3 Birth Time Rectification (Phase 5)
Advanced feature: the system asks the user to confirm 3-4 significant life events with dates (marriage, career change, loss, relocation). It computes which Lagna produces Dasha periods that align with those events. This is classical birth time rectification, and it gives the system a way to progressively refine the chart over time.
